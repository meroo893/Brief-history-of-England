Краят на една епоха

Смъртта на кралица Виктория, която е била символ на ценностите, величието и слабостта на възрастта, бележи повратна точка. При смъртта на кралицата синът ѝ Едуард VII идва на трона(1901-1910).

His age can be seen as a period of social changes. Important milestones in this process were the Education Act, which guaranteed secondary education, and legislation that recognized the right of Trade Union to strike.

Old age pensions and National Health Insurance were introduced and government offices, where people went to find jobs, were opened.

Britain's foreign policy

In the last decades of the 19th century, England's foreign policy had been one of 'splendid isolation'. Europe was then divided between France and Russia on one side, Germany, Austria and Italy on the other.

After the turn of the century, however, Britain became concerned about the expansion of the German Empire. In 1904, Britain and France, both frightened by Germany, signed a treaty of friendship. In 1907, this agreement became the Triple Entente, when France's ally, Russia, joined.

It was opposed by the Triple Alliance of Germany, Austria and Italy. On Edward VII's death, his son George V(1910-1936) came to the throne. His reign is often associated with the horrors of the First World War (1914-1918) and the sense of disillusionment that followed the conflict.

The First World War

Soon, half of the nations of the world became involved in the conflict, as well as the colonies of the British Empire. The Great War was a conflict of unparalleled destruction, in which six million died and twenty five million were maimed.

Finally, the exhausted Germans were compelled to ask for an armistice. A Peace Treaty was then signed at Versailles in June 1919, and in the same time the League of Nations was founded in Geneva with the aim of keeping peace and controlling difficult political situations all over the world.

It was opposed by the Triple Alliance of Germany, Austria and Italy. On Edward VII's death, his son George V(1910-1936) came to the throne. His reign is often associated with the horrors of the First World War (1914-1918) and the sense of disillusionment that followed the conflict.

Post-war years

The war caused severe economic consequences from 1919 to 1933, when over three million people in Britain ware unemployed.

All attempts at reconstruction seemed definitely shattered with the 1929 slump and the Wall Street Crash in the USA.

The whole of Europe as affected, and became caught in a grip of high inflation and big unemployment.

The British Commonwealth of Nations

The British Commonwealth of Nations began to take form in the early 20th century when the self-governing countries within the British Empire came together for Imperial Conferences.

In 1926, representatives of the principal parts of the Empire(South Africa, Canada, Australia, New Zealand and Newfoundland) defined the Commonwealth nations 'autonomous' but still 'united by a common allegiance to the Crown'.

The year of the three kings

The year of George V's death(1936) is known as the year of the Three Kings. The succession of George's son to the throne as Edward VIII was very short.

He abdicated and his brother George VI came to the throne. He reigned well and wisely having to face the problems that arose out of Britain's involvement

in the Second World War. His daughter, Elizabeth became Queen of England in 1952.

The rise of dictatorship

Dictatorships emerged during the 1920s and 1930s. Totalitarian governments found fertile ground for growth in Russia, Italy, Germany and other countries. In Russia, Communism came to power in the revolution of November 1917. In Italy, Mussolini founded the Fascist party in 1919.

He quickly seized all powers of government and transformed the country into a totalitarian state. In Germany, the National Socialist, or Nazi, party came to power in 1933.

Adolf Hitler crushed all political opposition and started to rebuild Germany's armed strength. In Japan, by 1931, the militarists began to dominate the government.

The Second World War(1939-1945)

The three main causes of the Second World War were:
- the problems left unsolved after WW1;
- the rise of dictatorship;
- the desire of Germany,Italy and Japan for more territory.

The war saw the Allied powers(France and Britain, and later Russia and the USA) against the Axis powers(Germany,Italy,Japan). In England the Conservative government was replaced by a coalition headed by Winston Churchill(1874-1965). His inspired leadership united the people if Britain against Nazi Germany and its allies.

He negotiated the alliance with the USSR and the USA and met Stalin and Roosevelt at three major conferences(in Teheran, 1943; in Yalta and Potsdam, both in 1945), in which the allies coordinated the major campaigns of the war and then discussed post-war settlements, by which the world was divided into 'Zones of Influence'.
